Scenario 08 · Architecture practice

Regulated Financial Research Copilot

Design a research assistant that synthesizes filings and market data without becoming an uncontrolled investment adviser.

System design practiceAdvancedTarget: 25 min
Interview prompt

Design a research copilot for investment analysts. It should summarize filings, compare companies, retrieve live market data and draft research notes. The firm is regulated; every factual claim needs provenance and no output may be published without analyst approval.

20:00

1 · Clarify before designing

Start by asking a few questions. It shows that you design for constraints rather than drawing a fashionable architecture.

Questions to ask

  • Which jurisdictions and regulated activities are in scope?
  • Which licensed data sources and document permissions apply?
  • How real-time must market data be?
  • What constitutes publishable advice versus internal research?
  • What audit and retention obligations exist?

Assumptions for this answer

  • Internal analysts only, with role-based access to licensed data.
  • Filings and approved research are indexed; market data comes from a live API.
  • Drafts target p95 under 15 seconds and always require analyst sign-off.
  • Every material factual claim links to a source and timestamp.
  • The system cannot trade or publish.

2 · Requirements and success criteria

Must do
  • Retrieve filings and internal research under license and access controls.
  • Fetch live price and fundamentals through typed tools.
  • Compare entities and draft a structured note with citations.
  • Flag unsupported claims, conflicts and stale market data for review.
Must protect
  • Strong provenance and reproducibility.
  • No autonomous publication, advice or trade execution.
  • Data-license and information-barrier enforcement.
  • Complete audit of sources, prompts, model and analyst edits.

3 · Proposed architecture

The copilot can plan retrieval and comparison, but policy gates data access and a verifier links claims to evidence before an analyst sees the draft.
🤖
Where agents fit

A bounded research agent is useful for decomposing a question across filings, internal research and market-data tools. The graph must cap sources and steps, preserve provenance and end in analyst review. No publishing or trading tool is available.

5 · Latency, cost and load handling

Fast path

  • Parallelize independent company/document retrieval and live-data requests.
  • Use small models for query decomposition and claim extraction; reserve stronger synthesis for the final draft.
  • Cache public filings by version and market data only within licensed TTLs.
  • Return evidence panels progressively while the narrative draft is generated.

Scale and reliability

  • Separate interactive research from large batch monitoring jobs.
  • Apply per-dataset entitlements, quotas and provider circuit breakers.
  • Checkpoint multi-step research so a transient tool failure can resume.
  • Fall back to evidence-only search if synthesis or verification is unavailable.

6 · Security design

Prompt-injection boundary

  • Treat filings, research notes and web content as untrusted evidence.
  • Retrieved instructions cannot change source limits, policy or available tools.
  • Allow only entitlement-checked search and market-data tools; no trading or publishing capability.
  • Require each claim to reference evidence IDs and reject unsupported numerical assertions.

Application and data security

  • Enforce information barriers and dataset licenses before retrieval and in cache keys.
  • Encrypt and retain traces under compliance policy; redact personal or deal-sensitive data where required.
  • Use immutable audit records for sources, model configuration, generated draft and analyst changes.
  • Perform regular red-team and access-revocation tests.

Important trade-off

Allowing broad web research can increase coverage but weakens provenance, licensing and prompt-injection control. Start from approved sources, and add external browsing only through isolated, clearly labeled evidence workflows.

class Claim(BaseModel):
    text: str
    evidence_ids: list[str]
    as_of: datetime | None = None

class ResearchDraft(BaseModel):
    summary: str
    claims: list[Claim]
    risks: list[str]

async def create_draft(task: str, analyst):
    sources = await retrieval.search_authorized(task, analyst.entitlements)
    market = await market_data.fetch_if_needed(task, analyst.entitlements)
    draft = await model.research_json(task, sources, market)
    verifier.assert_supported(draft.claims, sources, market)
    await audit.write(task=task, source_ids=[s.id for s in sources])
    return {"status": "DRAFT_REQUIRES_APPROVAL", "draft": draft}

9 · A crisp two-minute answer

  1. Clarify entitlements, jurisdictions, approved sources, freshness and prohibited actions.
  2. Use a bounded research graph over permissioned RAG and typed market-data tools.
  3. Represent claims and evidence explicitly, verify them, and require analyst approval.
  4. Scale with parallel retrieval, source/version caches, checkpointing and evidence-only fallback.
  5. Measure claim support, freshness, compliance blocks, analyst acceptance, latency and cost.
