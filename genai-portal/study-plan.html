Plan the whole hub without studying the same topic twice

Use this page before entering any course. It estimates the focused time required for every top-level section and gives a practical route for AI Engineer and GenAI interviews. The estimates assume you are starting with no Neural Networks, AI or GenAI background, while already knowing basic Python and some classical ML. They include explanation, note-making, coding, debugging, active recall and interview practice—not passive scrolling.

Your minimum study commitment

Target 2–4 focused hours each weekday and 8 focused hours across the weekend. That produces roughly 18–28 hours per week. At that pace, the recommended interview route takes approximately 12–26 weeks. A beginner should expect some topics—especially Transformers, Advanced RAG, LangGraph and production projects—to need a second pass.

Why the full-library total is much larger

The raw total is approximately 610–970 hours, but the Complete Interview Hub, Deep Dives and hands-on guides repeat or extend concepts from GenAI Mastery. For interview preparation, use them as targeted reinforcement rather than reading every overlapping page in sequence.

Recommended AI Engineer interview route

1

Neural foundations

50–75 hours. Start with Neural Networks for AI Engineers, then complete LLM Foundations, Transformers and Local LLMs.

2

Retrieval and RAG

60–90 hours. Study embeddings, vector databases, basic RAG and advanced RAG. Build at least one small retrieval application.

3

Agents, tools and MCP

65–100 hours. Learn the agent loop, tool calling, MCP, LangChain, LlamaIndex, LangGraph and multi-agent trade-offs.

4

Production and projects

80–125 hours. Cover evaluation, observability, security, latency and cost. Complete one project you can defend deeply.

5

Interview conversion

45–70 hours. Practise the question bank, focused coding labs and system-design scenarios aloud under time limits.

✓

Target outcome

You should be able to explain concepts simply, implement core patterns in Python, debug failure modes and defend architecture trade-offs without relying on memorized wording.

Three rules for using the plan

→

Follow dependencies

Neural networks and LLM foundations come before transformers, RAG and agents.

⌁

Skip duplicate reading

Use reference sections only when the primary curriculum leaves a gap or an interview exposes weakness.

⌨

Count active work

Hours count when you code, retrieve from memory, draw systems or answer aloud—not when a page is merely open.
