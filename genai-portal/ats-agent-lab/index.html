Applied agent engineering

Learn the real system, not a toy abstraction

This course reverse-engineers the six AI capabilities in the uploaded recruitment app and rebuilds their patterns in small, readable Python. You will learn the boundary between prompts, schemas, services, database writes, safety checks and human decisions.

🧭
A useful distinction before we begin

The app calls these components agents, but technically they are mostly bounded, single-task LLM workers: each receives prepared context and returns validated JSON. They do not run an open-ended ReAct tool loop. That is a good design choice for recruitment because it is easier to test, audit and keep under human control.

How the agents fit together

The app uses narrow AI workers at specific workflow moments. Every consequential transition remains a normal service-layer or human decision.
